# Screenshot Analysis Log

Purpose:
Preserve screenshot-based design readings produced during homepage and product-surface analysis.

This log exists because screenshot interpretation in design sessions is otherwise easy to lose. The page-state docs preserve machine-derived or structured page descriptions, but this file preserves the human visual reading:
- what stood out
- what was visible
- what looked stronger or weaker in practice
- what the screenshot proved about the product

This is a working analysis log, not a polished spec.

---

## Entry — 2026-03-20 — Compare page screenshot

### Screenshot subject
Desktop view of `/compare`, showing:
- page heading
- explanatory paragraph
- main compare module
- comparison between Data Science and Data Engineering
- visible TERM 1 through TERM 4

### What was visible
- standard top nav with Compare active
- large heading `Compare Degrees`
- plain explanatory text
- dark control strip with `Change` and `Reset`
- 3-part comparison summary band:
  - blue left block
  - dark shared block
  - gold/orange right block
- term bars and lane-based comparison content
- shared and unique courses with visible CU counts

### Key visual facts preserved
- the page is sparse and focused
- the comparison module dominates the page
- the 3-part summary header is visually strong
- term grouping makes the difference legible
- left/shared/right lane model is easy to understand
- the visualization is more persuasive than the intro copy
- the top control strip feels somewhat bolted-on

### What the screenshot proved
- Compare is homepage-worthy
- Compare is a flagship feature
- Compare demonstrates structured intelligence, not just a styled list
- Compare makes visible something the raw catalog does not support at all

### Planning implication
A screenshot crop from the compare module is likely better homepage material than the current homepage compare callout box.

## Entry — 2026-03-20 — Raw catalog BSCS screenshot

### Screenshot subject
Raw WGU catalog PDF entry for Bachelor of Science, Computer Science, spanning the bottom of one page and the top of the next.

### What was visible
- centered program title
- italic description paragraph
- one large continuous curriculum table
- columns:
  - CCN
  - Course Number
  - Course Description
  - CUs
  - Term
- rows spanning Terms 1–9
- no explicit term section headers
- page break during the roster
- final black total row:
  - BSCS
  - 202412
  - Total CUs: 117

### Key visual facts preserved
- the source is static and disclosure-oriented
- the roster is dense and flat
- terms are only represented by the last numeric column
- long course titles wrap awkwardly inside cells
- the entry is broken across pages
- there are no links, no sidebars, no contextual supports

### What the screenshot proved
- Atlas is solving a real source-format problem
- source information is useful but poorly structured for comparison and exploration
- Atlas degree pages are materially more navigable and interpretable than the source presentation

### Planning implication
The raw catalog screenshot is strong evidence for internal product framing but probably not a homepage image. Its main value is as baseline proof for messaging and product positioning.
