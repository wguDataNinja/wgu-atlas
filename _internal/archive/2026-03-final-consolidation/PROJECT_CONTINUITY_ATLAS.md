## Core project mission

Atlas exists to make canonical WGU information easier for students to use than the native catalog PDFs. The core value is not just collecting catalog data, but helping a student understand schools, degrees, and courses by browsing the entities relevant to them.

A student may arrive looking at only their own degree or course, but Atlas should let them gradually learn the broader structure of WGU through normal browsing:
	•	what school a program belongs to
	•	what courses are in a degree
	•	how programs relate to each other
	•	what official supporting resources help explain a program
	•	how degrees and courses have changed over time

Historical catalog context is one of Atlas’s most distinctive capabilities and may be unique to this product. It is valuable because it helps explain the current entity the student is viewing. It should remain a supporting differentiator, not the main identity of the site on core browse/detail surfaces.

Product success criteria

Atlas succeeds if it:
	•	presents official WGU information in a way that is easier to navigate, understand, and trust than long catalog PDFs
	•	helps students learn useful context through ordinary browsing, not only through specialist/history pages
	•	keeps provenance clear and Atlas interpretation visibly separate from source material
	•	remains explainable as a documented, reproducible project in a portfolio or interview setting
	•	stays simple enough to maintain and update with low ongoing operator effort

Maintenance constraint

Simplicity, reproducibility, and low-maintenance operation are core project constraints.

Prefer:
	•	deterministic exports over live inference
	•	stable review artifacts over hidden/manual judgment
	•	compact canonical docs over sprawling notes
	•	workflows that can be repeated with modest effort whenever a new catalog arrives

Avoid:
	•	feature directions that create heavy recurring maintenance
	•	systems that are difficult to explain simply
	•	unnecessary complexity that does not clearly improve student usefulness or trust
