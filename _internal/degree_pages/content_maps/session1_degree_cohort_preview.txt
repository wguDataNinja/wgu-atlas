  Every degree page renders in this section order:
  1.  Breadcrumb: Degrees › {code}
  2.  Header line: {code} · Current|Retired · N CUs
  3.  H1: canonical_name
  4.  School link (or plain text)
  5.  Retired notice (if retired)
  6.  GuideProvenance badge (if guide artifact exists)
  7.  About This Degree (if enriched.description exists)
  8.  Degree History (always present)
  9.  Learning Outcomes (if enriched.outcomes present)
  10. Licensure Preparation (if cert_signals has licensure entries)
  11. Industry Certifications (if cert_signals has professional_cert entries)
  12. Related Programs (if family present)
  13. Course Roster — normal OR suppressed block (if enriched.roster present)
  14. Areas of Study (if guide artifact has areas_of_study)
  15. Capstone (if capstone.present = true)
  16. Back link / footer nav

  Data sources:
  - Header/history/school: public/data/programs.json

  - All guide blocks: data/program_guides/degree_artifacts/{code}_degree_artifact.json

======================================================================
  CROSS-COHORT OBSERVATIONS
======================================================================

  1. AOS PLACEMENT IS CONSISTENTLY LATE
     On every full-roster program (BSCS, BSSWE, BSSESC, BSDA, BSITM), Areas of
     Study appears after the full course roster. Roster length ranges from 38–42
     courses / 9–10 terms. AoS — the richest guide content with descriptions and
     competency bullets — is at or near the bottom of a long page.
     Exception: MEDETID (12 courses, 4 terms) where AoS is near the top relatively.
     Exception: MATSPED where AoS IS the primary content (roster suppressed).

  2. AOS COURSE TITLES HAVE NO /COURSES/{CODE} LINKS
     Course Roster links every code (font-mono blue, /courses/{code}).
     Areas of Study shows only title + description + competency bullets — no code,
     no link. A student who sees "Introduction to Computer Science" in AoS cannot
     click to the course page. Inconsistent with the roster.

  3. MISSING OUTCOMES IS SILENT
     BSSESC has no outcomes in enriched data. The Learning Outcomes section does
     not appear. No placeholder, no explanation. The page moves from Degree History
     directly to Licensure Preparation. A student has no way to know if outcomes
     were omitted due to a data gap, or if outcomes are simply not published for
     this program type.

  4. CAVEAT PROMINENCE VARIES
     - MATSPED, MEDETID: amber pill in header (most visible)
     - BSITM: red text in badge + amber pill — two signals but badge is small
     - BSSWE: amber "medium confidence" in badge only — no pill — quietest signal
     Only the amber pill (present for MATSPED, MEDETID, BSITM) is likely to be
     noticed by a skimming reader.

  5. ADVISOR-GUIDED FRAMING IS THIN (BSSESC)
     The amber banner "Advisor-sequenced — individual pacing varies." is one line.
     It explains nothing about what advisor-sequenced means for a student's actual
     experience vs. a term-sequenced program.

  6. SUPPRESSED ROSTER (MATSPED) INVERTS THE PAGE
     MATSPED's suppressed block tells users the course list is "available below in
     Areas of Study." But Areas of Study groups don't behave like a course roster —
     they're collapsed accordions with descriptions and bullets. The user has to
     expand each of 7 groups to find all 30 courses. No aggregate view.

  7. CAPSTONE DOUBLE-MENTION (MEDETID)
     The capstone caveat appears twice: in the GuideProvenance header pill AND in
     the GuideCapstone block at the bottom. Both say the capstone is partial.
     Different wording, same substance — currently no attempt to deduplicate.

  8. BSITM CAPSTONE GAP
     "Capstone and Portfolio" exists as an AoS group (3 courses), but
     capstone.present=false so GuideCapstone block doesn't render. The capstone
     information is only accessible by expanding that specific AoS group.
     A student who doesn't open AoS won't know this program has a capstone.

  9. CU TOTALS OFTEN DIFFER BETWEEN ROSTER AND CATALOG
     Programs seen: BSCS (117 vs 123), BSSESC (124 vs 129), BSITM (113 vs 120).
     The page footer shows both and explains the difference, but this may be
     confusing to students who see "117 CUs" in the roster and "123 CUs" in
     the header badge.

  10. COLLEGE HISTORY IS PRESENT ON MOST BUT NOT ALL
     BSCS, BSSWE, BSSESC, MATSPED, BSDA: have rename chain (e.g., CIT → SoT)
     MEDETID, BSITM: single-college history, no chain rendered
     The chain renders as pill badges with arrows — clean, but without date ranges.

======================================================================
  OPEN DESIGN / POLICY QUESTIONS FOR SESSION 2
======================================================================

  Q1. AOS ORDER: Should Areas of Study move above the Course Roster?
      Arguments for: AoS has richer content; students researching what they'll
        learn should hit descriptions before a CU table.
      Arguments against: Course sequence (roster) is what students primarily want;
        AoS is supplemental enrichment; current order puts facts first.
      Current order: roster → AoS on all programs except MATSPED (suppressed).

  Q2. AOS COURSE LINKS: Should AoS course titles link to /courses/{code}?
      Arguments for: Roster links courses; AoS should too; students can navigate
        from description to full course page.
      Arguments against: AoS titles may not always match catalog codes exactly
        (they come from guide text, not catalog); linking requires code resolution.
      Current: no links in AoS.

  Q3. MISSING OUTCOMES: Should absent outcomes trigger a placeholder?
      E.g., "Program learning outcomes not yet available in this catalog edition."
      Affects: education programs (BSSESC and others) where outcomes are absent.

  Q4. ADVISOR-GUIDED FRAMING: Is the single amber line enough?
      Should advisor-guided programs have more explanation of what sequencing
      means vs. a self-paced term structure?

  Q5. PROVENANCE BADGE PROMINENCE: Is the small badge sufficient for quality signals?
      BSITM's red "low confidence" is the only red element — but it's in a tiny
      badge in the header. Should degraded-quality programs get a more prominent
      notice block?

  Q6. SUPPRESSED ROSTER NAVIGATION: Does MATSPED's page communicate clearly that
      AoS is the course catalog substitute?
      The footer says "see Areas of Study below" but AoS is collapsed by default.
      Should the suppressed block have a direct call-to-action or auto-expand?

  Q7. CAPSTONE DOUBLE-MENTION (MEDETID): Should the GuideCapstone block suppress
      the partial note when the same content appears in GuideProvenance? Or is
      redundancy acceptable for this edge case?

  Q8. CAPSTONE DISCOVERY (BSITM): Should programs with "capstone" in an AoS group
      but capstone.present=false get any visible capstone signal?
      Currently: nothing. The student must expand "Capstone and Portfolio" AoS group.

  Q9. ROSTER SOURCE LABEL: The roster badge says "WGU Catalog 2026-03" and AoS
      is labeled "WGU Program Guide." Should these source differences be more
      clearly explained so students understand which content is from which source?
